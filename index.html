A former Commerce Dept policy adviser (now at the Carnegie Endowment) argues the Lutnick letter is so poorly drafted it may not even restrict API/chatbot access — accessing Fable 5 / Mythos 5 through a digital interface, he says, may not qualify as an 'export' under the EAR.

His core point: the Export Administration Regulations were built for transferring controlled items/technology, and per Phillips-Robins, "users' accessing Fable 5 or Mythos 5 through a digital interface doesn't qualify as an 'export'" — so the Lutnick letter, as drafted, may not actually restrict the chatbot/API access it purports to. This adds a statutory-applicability question to the civil-liberties challenge FIRE raised (unconstitutional 'kill switch' / prior restraint), widening the legal doubts over the directive even though no lawsuit over the export controls has been filed.
