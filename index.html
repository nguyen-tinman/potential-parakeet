Pentagon moves two-thirds of AI workflows off Anthropic
The Department of War says it has moved at least two-thirds of its AI workflows off Anthropic's models and 'will no longer be single-threaded to one AI provider.'
Per Fortune (Jun 16, 10:57 AM ET), the Department of War announced Monday afternoon it 'will no longer be single-threaded to one AI provider' and has already shifted at least two-thirds of its AI workflows off Anthropic. Secretary of War Pete Hegseth posted Sunday: 'Three months ago, [the Department of War] kicked Anthropic out of our building — forever.' A concrete, dated escalation of the government's posture toward Anthropic during the suspension — distinct from the March supply-chain-risk contract bar and the Jun 13 DoD CIO statement — and one Fortune frames as a risk to Anthropic's confidential IPO.

Anthropic–Commerce meeting held — no deal yet
Anthropic's senior technical staff met Commerce officials (incl. Cyber Director Cairncross) Monday; negotiations continue but no restoration or deal was announced.
Per the Globe and Mail, Anthropic's senior technical staff met Commerce Department officials in Washington on Monday — with National Cyber Director Sean Cairncross attending — and have met with officials virtually every day since the directive. An Anthropic spokesperson said both sides 'were working quickly to reach a solution,' but no deal, restoration, or timeline emerged. Amodei and Lutnick are both set to attend the G7 in Evian-les-Bains, France, where they may speak as talks continue. (Corroborated by CNBC and the Washington Examiner.)

76 prominent security pros sign an open letter urging the government to lift the controls.
An open letter signed by ~76 cybersecurity experts — incl. Alex Stamos, Katie Moussouris, Rachel Tobac, Casey Ellis, Jon Callas, Paul Vixie, Dino Dai Zovi — urges the administration (Lutnick/Cairncross) to reverse the ban: 'To pull the best capabilities away from defenders without a good reason when our adversaries are rapidly advancing is dangerous.' The first organized expert pushback against the suspension.
