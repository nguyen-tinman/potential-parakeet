House letter: coordinate AI-found vulnerabilities, expand defensive Mythos access
A month BEFORE the suspension, Reps. Bob Latta (R) & Doris Matsui (D) and 33 colleagues asked National Cyber Director Sean Cairncross for a federal plan to handle the flood of AI-discovered software flaws — and to EXPAND 'controlled defensive access' to tools like Anthropic's Mythos.
Sent May 13, 2026 to ONCD Director Sean Cairncross by a bipartisan group (35 signatories; Latta + Matsui leading), the 'AI-Discovered Vulnerability Coordination Letter' was precipitated by Mythos surfacing thousands of high-severity zero-days across major operating systems and browsers. It asked the administration to: make CISA the lead agency for AI-vulnerability coordination; build a federal plan to manage the disclosure volume; convene agencies + industry on disclosure procedures; EXPAND controlled defensive access to frontier vulnerability-discovery models (Mythos, GPT-5.5-Cyber) for vetted defenders and OSS maintainers; fund federal patching help; and set rules for restricting proof-of-concept exploits. It reads as an acute irony in hindsight: “AI can empower defenders to discover a multitude of serious vulnerabilities, but the corresponding disclosure, validation, patching, and deployment efforts may struggle to keep pace.” — lawmakers wanted MORE defensive Mythos access weeks before the June 12 directive cut it off entirely.
